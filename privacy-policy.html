4. Information We Collect

4.1 Account Information

When you sign in using Google Sign-In during onboarding, we receive the following information from Google:

  • Your Google account display name;
  • Your Google account email address;
  • Your Google account profile photo URL;
  • A unique Firebase User ID (UID) assigned by Firebase Authentication.

We store your name, email, and Firebase UID in our Firestore database to maintain your user profile. Your profile photo is not stored by us — it is fetched directly from Google's servers when displayed in the App.

4.2 Phone Number

During onboarding, you are asked to provide your personal phone number. This number is:

  • Stored in your user profile in our Firestore database;
  • Used solely to identify your account and support account recovery;
  • Never shared with third parties.

4.3 Call Data (Metadata Only)

When the App blocks an incoming call, the following metadata is recorded:

  • The phone number of the incoming caller;
  • The date and time of the call;
  • The blocking action taken (blocked / silenced);
  • The filter rule type that triggered the block (e.g. prefix match, country match).

Important: We never record, access, intercept, or store the audio content of any call. The App only processes the phone number and metadata associated with an incoming call.

4.4 Filter Rules

Blocking rules you create within the App — including number prefixes, suffixes, full numbers, country selections, time-based rules, and regex patterns — are stored exclusively on your device in a local database. These rules are never uploaded to our servers.

4.5 Device and Technical Information

We collect the following technical information about your device and store it in your user profile in Firestore:

  • Device model and manufacturer;
  • Android OS version;
  • App version number;
  • Country (derived from your device locale);
  • App installation date;
  • Last active timestamp.

This information is used solely to provide support, debug issues, and understand compatibility across devices.

4.6 Subscription Information

If you purchase a paid subscription (Plus or Premium), Google Play Billing processes your payment. We do not receive or store your payment card details, billing address, or any financial information. We only receive a confirmation of your subscription tier and its validity period from Google Play. This confirmation is stored in two places: encrypted local storage on your device, and your user profile in Firebase Firestore — solely to allow your subscription to be restored if you reinstall the App on a new device.

8. Community Spam Database

SpamCrusher includes a community-powered spam protection feature. When the App blocks an incoming call that is identified as spam (excluding calls blocked by Do Not Disturb time rules or personal whitelist rules), the following data is submitted to the Community Spam Database hosted in Firebase Realtime Database:

  • The phone number of the blocked caller, stored in E.164 international format;
  • A running count of how many times that number has been reported across all App users;
  • The date and time of the most recent report;
  • The category of filter rule that triggered the block (e.g. prefix, country).

Note on caller privacy: The phone numbers submitted to the Community Spam Database are numbers of individuals or entities that have been identified as spam callers by our blocking engine. These numbers are not associated with any App user's identity. The purpose of this database is exclusively to improve spam detection for all users. We do not use this database to build profiles, track individuals, or share data commercially.

This feature operates automatically whenever a qualifying call is blocked. It cannot be disabled separately. If you do not wish for blocked caller numbers to be submitted to the Community Spam Database, you should not use the App.

14. Data Security

We implement the following technical and organisational measures to protect your Personal Data:

  • Encryption at rest: Subscription tier and sensitive local data are stored using Android's EncryptedSharedPreferences (AES-256-GCM), backed by the device's hardware security module where available.
  • Encryption in transit: All communications between the App and Firebase servers use TLS (Transport Layer Security).
  • Firebase App Check: We use Google Play Integrity API via Firebase App Check to ensure that only our genuine, unmodified App can access our Firebase backend. This prevents unauthorised clients from reading or writing data.
  • Firebase Security Rules: Access to your Firestore profile data is restricted to your authenticated user account only. No other user can read or modify your profile.
  • Minimal data collection: We collect only the data strictly necessary for the App to function.

No method of electronic storage or transmission is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your Personal Data, we cannot guarantee absolute security. In the event of a data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and the relevant authorities as required by applicable law.
